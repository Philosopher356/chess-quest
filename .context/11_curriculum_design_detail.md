# Detailed Curriculum Design

The full pedagogical design for each chapter as originally planned. This guided the lesson content creation.

---

## WORLD 1: "THE KINGDOM" (Absolute Beginner)

### Chapter 1: The Chessboard
- **Lesson 1.1: "The Battlefield"** -- Board appears empty. Child taps squares and they light up. Mascot explains this is where the battle happens. Animated zoom from castle overview down to the board.
- **Lesson 1.2: "Ranks and Files"** -- Ranks highlight one-by-one as child swipes up/down. Files highlight left/right. Mini-game: "Tap the Square!" -- Sir Hops calls out "Find d4!" and child must tap the correct square.
- **Lesson 1.3: "Light and Dark"** -- Board starts all one color. Child drags paint buckets to color squares. Exercise: "What color is e5?" rapid-fire quiz.
- **Mastery gate**: Score 8/10 on a "Tap the Square" speed challenge.

### Chapter 2: The Pawns
- **Lesson 2.1: "The Brave Soldiers"** -- Single pawn appears. Child drags it forward. Valid squares glow green. Invalid glow red with shake animation.
- **Lesson 2.2: "The First Charge"** -- Pawn on starting rank. Show two-square option. Exercise: Move 4 pawns, choosing single or double.
- **Lesson 2.3: "Capturing Diagonally"** -- Pawn faces enemy diagonally. Arrow shows capture direction. Pieces that cannot be captured are grayed out.
- **Lesson 2.4: "Pawn Maze"** -- Mini-game: Pawn navigates rank 2 to rank 8, capturing specific enemies. Three stars based on move count.
- **Mastery gate**: Complete 3 pawn mazes with at least 2 stars.

### Chapter 3: The Rook
- **Lesson 3.1: "The Castle Tower"** -- Rook in center. Lines radiate showing all moves. Obstacles (walls) show rook cannot jump.
- **Lesson 3.2: "Rook Capture Mission"** -- 5 enemy pawns scattered. Rook captures in minimum moves. Star rating for efficiency.
- **Lesson 3.3: "Rook vs Rook"** -- Capture opponent's rook before it captures yours. Introduces piece value intuitively.
- **Mastery gate**: Capture all targets in 3 scenarios within target move count.

### Chapter 4: The Bishop
- **Lesson 4.1: "The Diagonal Runner"** -- Bishop on light square. All diagonal paths glow. Child discovers it stays on one color.
- **Lesson 4.2: "Bishop Capture Mission"** -- Same format as rook but diagonal. Stars for efficiency.
- **Lesson 4.3: "Two Bishops Team Up"** -- Both bishops show they cover all colors together. Alternate captures.
- **Mastery gate**: Efficiency challenge with the bishop.

### Chapter 5: The Queen
- **Lesson 5.1: "The Mighty Queen"** -- Rook lines and bishop diagonals light up simultaneously. Child explores freely.
- **Lesson 5.2: "Queen Domination"** -- Place the queen to attack maximum marked squares. Optimal solution puzzle.
- **Lesson 5.3: "The Queen's Mission"** -- Capture 8 targets in minimum moves.
- **Mastery gate**: Complete domination puzzles.

### Chapter 6: The Knight
- **Lesson 6.1: "Sir Hops Reveals His Secret"** -- The mascot IS a knight. Animated L-shape. Valid landing squares pulse. L-shape traced as trail.
- **Lesson 6.2: "Jumping Over Walls"** -- Board has obstacles. Knight is only piece shown jumping. Contrast with blocked rook.
- **Lesson 6.3: "Knight's Tour Challenge"** -- Visit 8-12 target squares (simplified, not full 64).
- **Lesson 6.4: "Knight Fork Preview"** -- Two enemy pieces placed so knight attacks both. "Sir Hops attacks TWO at once!"
- **Mastery gate**: Navigate knight to 5 targets in prescribed order.

### Chapter 7: The King
- **Lesson 7.1: "The King"** -- One square any direction. Crown glow effect. Slow, deliberate animation.
- **Lesson 7.2: "The King Cannot Be Captured"** -- Enemy rook threatens king. Red danger squares shown. King must move to safe square.
- **Lesson 7.3: "King Walk"** -- Navigate king across board avoiding enemy attack zones (red-tinted squares).
- **Mastery gate**: Navigate king safely through 3 danger mazes.

## WORLD 2: "THE RULES OF BATTLE" (Beginner)

### Chapter 8: Board Setup
- Drag each piece to starting square. Incorrect placements bounce back with hint. "The queen goes on her own color!"
- Speed setup timed challenge. Leaderboard against own best time.

### Chapter 9: Check
- Three escape methods demonstrated interactively: move king, block, capture attacker.
- "Find the Check" - board position shown, find which move gives check.
- "Escape the Check" - all three methods tested.

### Chapter 10: Checkmate
- Show check where NO escape exists. Animated celebration.
- "Checkmate in 1" puzzle series (20 puzzles, adaptive).
- "Is it Checkmate?" - classify positions as check/checkmate/neither.

### Chapter 11: Stalemate and Draws
- Position where NOT check but no legal moves. "It's a draw!"
- "Avoid the Stalemate" - winning position, must NOT stalemate.
- Other draws: insufficient material, threefold repetition, 50-move rule.

### Chapter 12: Special Moves
- **Castling**: Both kingside/queenside. Scenarios where NOT allowed. "Can white castle?" quiz.
- **En Passant**: Ghost pawn animation. "Spot the en passant" boards.
- **Pawn Promotion**: Glowing selection of Q/R/B/N. Underpromotion puzzle.

### Chapter 13: Piece Values
- Balance scale visual. Drag pieces onto scale. Pawn=1, N/B=3, R=5, Q=9, K=priceless.
- "Good Trades and Bad Trades" - exchange evaluator with material count.

## WORLD 3: "THE STRATEGIST" (Intermediate)

### Chapter 14: Opening Principles
- Center control with gold squares. Piece mobility comparison from center vs edge.
- Development rules: knights before bishops, don't move same piece twice, don't bring queen early.
- Castle early demonstration. Opening explorer (Italian, London, Queen's Gambit).
- **Evaluation**: Play opening against weak AI, scored by rubric (center/development/castling), NOT win/loss.

### Chapters 15-18: Tactics
- Each tactic taught with: concept explanation → interactive demo → progressive puzzles.
- Forks: 15 puzzles (knight, pawn, bishop, queen forks).
- Pins: absolute vs relative, 15+ puzzles.
- Skewers: reverse of pin concept, 10 puzzles.
- Discovered attacks: "surprise" effect animation.
- Double check: only king can move.
- Deflection/decoy: sacrifice to lure defenders.
- Overloading: "sweating" cartoon on overloaded piece.
- Mixed tactics trainer: randomized from all categories, adaptive difficulty.

### Chapter 19: Checkmate Patterns
- Back rank, Scholar's mate (+ defense!), smothered mate.
- Pattern flash drill with spaced repetition.

---

## WORLD 4: "THE GRANDMASTER'S PATH" (Advanced)

### Chapters 20-23: Strategy & Endgames
- Pawn structure: colored auras on isolated/doubled/backward/passed pawns. Chain attack.
- Piece activity: mobility counter comparison. "Improve your worst piece."
- King safety: pawn shield meter. Attack building with piece coordination.
- Endgames: guided K+Q and K+R technique, opposition concept, square rule visualization.

---

## WORLD 5: "THE MASTER'S DOMAIN" (Expert)

### Chapters 24-28: Mastery
- Complex endgames: Lucena/Philidor positions.
- Positional play: prophylaxis, space advantage, pawn breaks.
- Calculation: board disappears for blind calculation. Candidate moves (CCT method).
- Pattern recognition: 500+ puzzles with spaced repetition, time pressure.
- Complete games: guess-the-move format through famous games (Opera Game).
